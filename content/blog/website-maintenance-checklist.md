If you own a work truck, you do not wait for the engine to blow up before you change the oil. You check the dipstick, flush the radiator, and check the brake pads every few months. 

If you ignore maintenance, you end up stuck on the side of the road with a blown gasket, a cargo bed full of pipes, and a massive towing invoice. 

Websites work the same way. If you ignore them for six months, the plugins get old, the database gets sluggish, and eventually, the checkout stops working. 

Let's look at the direct answer first.

**A standard website maintenance checklist requires weekly database backups and security scans, monthly plugin and theme updates, quarterly contact form and speed checks, and annual domain and hosting renewals to prevent system downtime.**

Here is the preventative service checklist every business owner should follow.

---

## 1. Weekly Tasks (The Fluid Check)

These are the quick checks to make sure the core systems are running.

*   **Verify Backups:** Ensure your automated backup system is saving files to a secure cloud server (like AWS or Google Drive). If the site goes down, you need a recovery file.
*   **Monitor Uptime:** Use a free tool (like Uptime Robot) to alert you if the site goes down. 
*   **Security Logs:** Check for unauthorized login attempts or malicious activity.

---

## 2. Monthly Tasks (The Oil Change)

This is the technical servicing that keeps the gears turning.

*   **Run Updates:** Update the WordPress core, themes, and plugins. (Always run a backup *before* you click update, in case a plugin conflict crashes the layout.)
*   **Test Contact Forms:** Submit a dummy lead through your contact form. Make sure the notification email lands in your inbox, not your spam folder.
*   **Scan for Broken Links:** Use a free link checker to find and fix dead links.

---

## 3. Quarterly Tasks (The Brake Check)

Deep checks to keep your performance high.

*   **Audit Site Speed:** Run your URL on Google PageSpeed. If speed has dropped below 90, compress new images and clear server cache files.
*   **Database Cleanup:** Delete old draft pages, spam comments, and database revisions that bloat the server.
*   **Check SSL Status:** Make sure your security certificate is renewing automatically.

---

## 4. Annual Tasks (The Rego & Insurance)

Long-term management to prevent losing your digital assets.

*   **Domain & Hosting Renewal:** Ensure your credit card details are up to date on your registrar account. (Losing a domain because of an expired card is an expensive headache.)
*   **Review Content:** Update old pricing, address changes, team photos, and legal policies.
