If you are planning to sell products online in India, you are probably staring at two names: Shopify and WooCommerce. 

It is the classic e-commerce debate. 

Let's look at the direct answer first.

**Shopify is a managed, all-in-one SaaS platform that is easier to set up and secure but charges recurring subscription and transaction fees, whereas WooCommerce is a free, open-source WordPress plugin that offers complete customization and lower fees but requires you to manage hosting and security updates.**

Let's look at the plumbing of both systems and see which one fits your store.

---

## The Premium Shopping Mall vs The Standalone Street Shop

Think of Shopify like renting a shop inside a premium shopping mall. 

The mall handles the security, keeps the lights on, and sweeps the floors. It is easy to set up. You just put your products on the shelves and start selling. 

But you have to pay the mall a monthly rent. You also have to pay them a transaction fee on every sale. And if you want to repaint the storefront or change the checkout layout, the mall says no. You have to play by their rules.

WooCommerce is like buying a standalone commercial building on a street. 

You own the building. You do not pay transaction fees on your sales (except to your payment gateway). You can design the shop layout, add custom features, and change anything you want. 

But if the tap leaks, or if someone tries to break in, you are responsible. You have to hire a professional website development company to keep the server secure and the checkout running.

---

## The Hidden Transaction Fees

Shopify makes money by charging a monthly subscription. But they also charge a transaction fee of 0.5% to 2% if you do not use their internal payment system (Shopify Payments, which has limited support in India). 

If your store does ₹5,00,000 in monthly sales, that transaction fee adds up to ₹10,000 every single month. 

WooCommerce is free. You only pay the standard payment gateway charge (like Razorpay or Cashfree, which is around 2%). You save the extra transaction fee. 

---

## The Maintenance Burden

Let's talk about the grease in the pipes (the plugins). 

WooCommerce runs on WordPress. This means you need plugins for payment gateways, tax calculation, and inventory management. If one of those plugins updates and breaks another, your checkout stops working. 

A client in Coimbatore had his checkout break during a holiday sale. He lost ₹40,000 in sales before calling a web development company to fix the database conflict. 

Shopify has zero maintenance. They handle server updates, security patches, and database backups. Your site never breaks due to a core update.

---

## Rule of Thumb

If you have a limited budget, want complete control over your code, and already run a WordPress site, use WooCommerce. Just make sure you budget for professional maintenance packages to keep it secure.

If you want a hassle-free system that never goes down, has high-speed checkouts, and you do not mind paying monthly fees, choose Shopify.
