Imagine walking into a local hardware shop. You want to buy a pipe cutter. But when you walk inside, the lights are turned off. The shop assistant is sleeping under the counter. And there is a massive pile of cardboard boxes blocking the aisle to the tools. 

What do you do? You turn around, walk out, and go to the shop across the street. 

A lot of business websites do the exact same thing to online visitors. 

They pay a website design company to build a site, get traffic from Google, but get zero phone calls. 

Let's look at the direct answer first.

**Your website is not generating leads because of three main conversion leaks: slow page load speeds (over 3 seconds), high friction in contact forms (asking for too much information), and a lack of a clear, clickable Call to Action (CTA) above the fold.**

Here is how to wake up the silent shopkeeper on your homepage.

---

## 1. The Slow Load Leak (The Clogged Pipe)

Let's talk about the aerator job again. 

I once fixed a kitchen tap with "no pressure" in 90 seconds by rinsing the mesh aerator under water. It was just gunk blocking the flow. 

Slow websites have code gunk. 

If your developer uploaded a 10MB raw photo of your team to the hero section, the site speed collapses. Visitors on mobile connections will click the back button before the page loads. 

Verify your site on Google PageSpeed. If your score is red (under 50), your pipe is clogged. You need to compress images and clean the script loading order.

---

## 2. High Friction Forms (The Red Tape Trap)

If a customer wants a quote, do not ask them for their fax number, their postal code, and their business registration details. 

Every extra input field you add to a form reduces conversions by 10%. 

Keep it simple:
*   Name.
*   Phone Number.
*   What do you need?

That is it. You can gather the rest of the details on the phone call.

---

## 3. Hidden Contact Details (The Locked Door)

If a user has to scroll to the bottom of the page, click a "Contact" link, and search for a small email address just to call you, they won't do it. 

Place a large, bold button at the top right of every page that says "Call Now" or "Book Consultation." Make sure it is a clickable link (`tel:` or a WhatsApp chat link) so they can connect in one tap.

---

## 4. No Social Proof (The Trust Leak)

Why should a customer hire you instead of the other guy? 

If your homepage has zero testimonials, zero project photos, and generic text claiming you provide "quality services," you look like a scam. 

Display:
*   Before-and-after photos of actual jobs.
*   Screenshots of Google reviews.
*   Details of your licenses and insurance.

---

## Rule of Thumb

Before you spend money on search ads, fix your conversion leaks. Bringing traffic to a slow website is like pouring water into a bucket with a hole in the bottom.
