If you talk to agencies, they will use terms like "visual brand integration" and "backend database synthesis" to explain how a website is made. 

It sounds like they are trying to sell you a sci-fi spacesuit. 

Let's look at the direct answer first.

**Website design focuses on the visual layout, branding, and user experience (UI/UX) created using design software like Figma, while website development is the technical coding (using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and databases) that makes those designs functional on a web server.**

Let's look at the plumbing of website design vs website development so you know who to hire.

---

## The Architect vs The Builder

Think of website design like hiring an architect. 

The architect sits down with you, drafts the blueprints, decides where the bedrooms go, curates the colors, and plans the lighting. They make sure the house looks beautiful and feels spacious. This is **website design**. The designer uses tools like Figma to create mockups of your homepage.

Think of website development like hiring a builder. 

The builder takes the architect's blueprints. They dig the trench, pour the concrete slab, raise the structural timber, run the copper water pipes, and wire the light switches. This is **website development**. The developer writes code to turn the static Figma mockups into clickable, high-performance web pages.

---

## Why you need both (The Rusted Door Trap)

I have seen business owners hire a graphic designer to build their website. The designer creates a stunning visual mockup, but since they do not know how to code, they build the site using a slow drag-and-drop page builder. 

The result? The site looks great, but it takes 8 seconds to load on a mobile phone, and the contact form leaks emails into a spam folder. It is the digital equivalent of a beautiful front door that is rusted shut.

I have also seen developers build websites without a designer. The site loads in 200 milliseconds, but it looks like a spreadsheet from 1995. Customers take one look and leave because it doesn't look professional.

To get a professional business website, you need both a designer (who understands user psychology) and a developer (who writes clean, lightweight code).

---

## The Cost Split

When you review a quote from a professional website development company, the budget is split between design and development.

1.  **UI/UX Design Phase:** Mockup creation, typography selection, custom branding assets, and mobile layouts. (Takes about 35% of the budget.)
2.  **Coding & Integration Phase:** Frontend CSS styling, database setup, form validation, and server deployment. (Takes about 65% of the budget.)

---

If you are choosing a website design company, ask if they write clean code from scratch or if they just use templates. A good team should have distinct specialists for design and coding.
